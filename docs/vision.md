# BaseYield Vision

## Core Frame

Financial systems are built in layers:

1. Capital
2. Credit
3. Financial products

BaseYield applies this structure to crypto-native markets:

- **Bitcoin** as digital capital
- **STRC** as digital credit
- **Layer-3 wrappers** as programmable digital money and digital yield

## Why This Matters

Many capital markets still rely on opaque intermediaries, slow settlement, and rigid product packaging.  
Crypto infrastructure enables a more explicit and programmable model:

- Credit exposure can be represented digitally
- Product rules can be expressed in software
- Liquidity and yield tradeoffs can be made transparent to users

## The Opportunity

If STRC becomes a credible digital credit primitive, then a new product category becomes possible:

- money-like wrappers for users prioritizing stability and liquidity
- yield-oriented wrappers for users accepting more volatility and lockups

BaseYield explores how to make this configurable on Base with clear controls around reserve levels, liquidity profile, and risk posture.

## Product Direction

BaseYield is not positioned as a generic yield farm.  
It is intended as early infrastructure for a layered architecture:

- **Digital capital** anchors value
- **Digital credit** enables credit formation
- **Digital money and digital yield** provide user-facing utility

## Current Scope

This repository is a prototype and thesis demonstration:

- conceptual product modes
- simulated analytics
- contract skeleton for vault wrappers
- architecture and roadmap documentation

The goal is to show strategic direction and technical credibility, not a production financial service.
